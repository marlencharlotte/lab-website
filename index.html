Interventional Precision Genomics

We work at the interface of rare disease genomics and therapeutic translation, developing frameworks, tools, and strategies that help identify interventional opportunities for individuals with genetic disease.

About the lab

From genomic diagnosis to therapeutic possibility

The team has two core aims: 1) Expanding the genetic therapy toolbox by developing new therapeutic strategies to target rare genetic disorders, and 2) Developing computational tools to accelerate target identification and prioritisation of cases for genetic therapy development. The Lauffer Lab uses a combination of wet-lab and dry-lab-based methods to achieve its goals and aims to build a multidisciplinary team bridging clinical expertise, wet-lab research, and computational work. The Lauffer Lab plays a leading role in multiple international collaborations striving to incorporate (individualized) genetic therapies into routine medical practices. These include the Medical Research Council (MRC) Centre of Research Excellence (CoRE) in Therapeutic Genomics (https://genomicmedicine.ac.uk ) and the N=1 Collaborative (https://www.n1collaborative.org ), which is a global initiative to standardize and optimize individualized genetic therapy developments.
