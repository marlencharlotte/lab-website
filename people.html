Lab Lead

Marlen Lauffer

Principal Investigator

Marlen Lauffer is a physician-scientist trained in medicine, biochemistry and human molecular genetics. After obtaining her doctorate from the University of Munich, she spent time at the Erasmus MC Rotterdam and the University of Cologne before taking on a senior postdoc position at the Dutch Center for RNA Therapeutics in Leiden, where she helped establish the group from 2021 to 2026. Since February 2026, Marlen is leading the Interventional Precision Genomica Lab at the Institute of Developrmental and Regenerative Medicine at the University of Oxford. Her research aims to standardise the identification of patients who may benefit from emerging therapeutic approaches such as antisense oligonucleotides, gene editing, or gene replacement by developing international consensus frameworks and computational pipelines that enable rapid assessment. Marlen is involved in national and international efforts to advance the field of individualised therapies and leads initiatives within the globally active N=1 Collaborative. She has a background in medicine, biochemistry, and human molecular genetics and has held research positions in Germany, the Netherlands, and the United Kingdom. Her work combines genomic analysis, clinical translation, and community collaboration to accelerate pathways from genetic diagnosis to genetic therapy.
